# Way2Move — Brand Identity

**Completed:** April 2026
**Process:** Follows the brand identity framework used for Way2Fly — Phase 1 (Discovery) through Phase 4 (System). Adapted for a movement-first wellness/performance hybrid product.

---

## 1. Brand Positioning

**One sentence:** Way2Move is the movement operating system for normal-life athletes — it helps people who work a normal job train across disciplines, fix compensations, and understand their body from the ground up.

**What it is NOT:**
- A generic workout tracker (the market has fifty of those)
- A nutrition-first app (MyFitnessPal owns that territory)
- A sleep tracker (Oura, Whoop)
- A competitive strength-training log (Strong, Hevy)
- A yoga / meditation app (Headspace, Down Dog)
- A running tracker (Strava)

**What makes it different:**
- PRI/DNS-rooted corrective programming — nobody else in consumer apps starts from the pelvis, ribcage, and gait cycle
- A single place for training + recovery + nutrition + sleep + journaling — voice-first so the friction is near zero
- Body awareness is the primary signal — a compensation profile that evolves from your journal entries, weekly pulse, and AI assessment, not just your reps
- On-device video movement assessment (Phase 2) — pose estimation runs locally, no server, no subscription
- Motivating, not guilty — missed-a-day banners remind you of your goal, not your streak
- Built by a normal-life athlete who uses it, not a startup chasing a market

## 2. Brand Personality

**In one line:** "The physio you wish you had, in your pocket."

**The two defining moments:**

1. **The morning check-in.** Someone opens the app, taps the mic, and says: "Slept okay, low back is tight, I want to do the posture reset session today." The app parses this — logs the sleep, updates the compensation profile, and pulls up the session. This needs to feel **quiet, attentive, smart without being flashy**. No hype. No confetti. Just understanding.

2. **The progress moment.** Three weeks in, someone opens the compensation profile and sees "Anterior pelvic tilt" move from *active* to *improving*. The body map subtly softens that region's color. The goal linked to it shows 60% complete. This needs to feel **earned, calm, and real**. A quiet confirmation — not a badge explosion.

**Way2Move IS:**
- Built by someone who actually trains, rehabs, and journals
- Rooted in movement science (PRI, DNS, gait mechanics) without being academic
- Calm and confident — doesn't need to yell to be trusted
- Warm and organic — feels closer to a physical therapist's office than a gym
- Voice-first because typing while recovering is silly

**Way2Move is NOT:**
- Bro-culture, gym-bro, "rise and grind"
- Clinical, sterile, medical-app
- Toy-like or gamified to the point of triviality
- Streak-shaming or guilt-inducing
- Pretending to be a medical device

**Brand archetype:** The Sage/Healer — guides, restores, teaches. Has calm authority. Patient. Corrects without judgment.

**Contrast with Way2Fly:**
| Axis | Way2Fly | Way2Move |
|---|---|---|
| Theme | Action sport | Movement wellness |
| Mood | Bold, electric, performance | Calm, grounded, restorative |
| Default mode | Dark-first | Light-first (dark supported) |
| Personality | The Coach | The Sage |
| Color feel | Electric teal on deep space | Terracotta on warm linen |
| Typeface feel | Geometric, tech-forward | Humanist, editorial warmth |
| Motion feel | Snap, spring, celebration | Breath, flow, settle |
| Reward moment | Amber XP burst | Quiet green check + soft scale |

Both are from the same "Way2" family but speak to completely different use-contexts. Way2Fly is worn at the DZ with cold hands. Way2Move is opened on the couch in the morning, barefoot.

### Color Usage Rules

1. **Terracotta is the workhorse.** Every interactive element (primary buttons, links, active nav, progress bars filling up) uses terracotta. It's the "you can tap this" color.

2. **Sage is the "your body is listening" color.** It confirms body-aware outcomes: improving compensations, resolved patterns, gut feeling in a healthy range, journal entry saved. Sage is calm. It says "good — noticed, logged." Never used for CTAs.

3. **Soft Gold is rare.** It ONLY shows up on milestones worth remembering: goal achieved, first assessment complete, compensation fully resolved, program finished. Because it appears maybe once a month, it lands.

4. **Never use pure white (#FFFFFF) for surfaces.** Use Off-White `#FFFDFA` — adds barely-perceptible warmth that pairs with the cream text color and makes pure-white screenshots feel clinical by comparison.

5. **Never use pure black (#000000) for dark-mode backgrounds.** Warm Charcoal `#1A1612` has a whisper of brown-red that keeps the night mode feeling organic, not electronic.

6. **Compensation severity is a gradient, not a status.**
   - Resolved: Mist Sage `#C4D5BE`
   - Improving: Sage `#7A9B76`
   - Mild: Honey `#D99434`
   - Moderate: Terracotta `#C4622D`
   - Significant: Clay Red `#BE4A3A`

## 4. Typography

### Display typeface: Fraunces

**Why Fraunces (not a geometric sans like Way2Fly's Plus Jakarta):**

Way2Fly is action sport — geometric sans works. Way2Move is movement wellness rooted in body awareness and journaling. It needs warmth, a hint of editorial thoughtfulness, and a voice that sounds like a physio's handwritten note, not a Silicon Valley dashboard.

Fraunces is a variable serif with organic curves, subtle ink traps, and a quiet personality. It handles display sizes beautifully (hero numbers, greetings, journal titles) and brings a human, almost analog quality that fits a body awareness product. Used only for display — not body — so readability is never at risk.

**Usage:** Greeting headers, journal entry titles, hero numbers (streak count, total sessions), screen titles on signature screens (home, compensation profile, progress photos), quote callouts, milestone moments.

**Weights used:**
| Weight | Use |
|---|---|
| 400 | Italic display body (rare, for journaling hero text) |
| 600 | Section-level display headers |
| 700 | Screen titles, greetings |

### UI typeface: Manrope

**Why Manrope:**

Manrope is a modern humanist sans-serif with warm, round terminals. It sits between the cold geometry of Inter/Plus Jakarta and the character of Fraunces — meaning it harmonizes with Fraunces without feeling like a sibling. Excellent legibility at small sizes, wide weight range, free via Google Fonts.

**Usage:** All UI, all body text, buttons, labels, navigation, timestamps — everything that is not display.

**Weights used:**
| Weight | Use |
|---|---|
| 400 | Body text, descriptions, meta |
| 500 | Supporting labels, input placeholders |
| 600 | Button text, section labels, navigation |
| 700 | Card titles, emphasis |
| 800 | Display numbers when paired with Fraunces would feel too editorial |

## 5. Logo System

### The Mark: "The Rooted 2"

**Concept:** A stylized "2" constructed as a single continuous path — a long, solid horizontal base (the ground), a diagonal rise (the step), and a calm, slightly asymmetric arch at the top (the body's arc in motion). Unlike Way2Fly's tilted, airflow-swept 2, this 2 is upright and grounded.

**Why this won (over alternatives):**

| Concept | Strength | Weakness | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| Rooted 2 | Consistent with Way2 family; conveys foundation + upward movement; single stroke = scales well | Another "2" — risk of feeling same as Way2Fly | **SELECTED** — distinct enough via stance/proportions |
| Gait cycle dots | Unique, maps directly to content | Reads as "3 dots" at small sizes, not as a logo | Rejected |
| Standing wave (M + arc) | Beautiful, abstract | Doesn't read as "Way2Move"; loses name connection | Rejected |
| Barefoot footprint | Body-forward, literal | Too literal; dates poorly; reads as children's app | Rejected |
| Horizontal body stack | Anatomy-based (spine + pelvis + head as abstraction) | Too complex; hard at 48px | Rejected |

**Construction:**
- **Base line:** horizontal, longer than Way2Fly's, anchored from left edge to right edge (full width). Represents the ground — stable, uninterrupted, foundational.
- **Diagonal:** a clean straight line rising from the base's right end up-and-left. Represents the step, the ascent, the rise.
- **Arch:** a gentle, asymmetric curve arcing from the diagonal's top down to a point slightly left of the base's left end. The left terminal sits *just below* the peak height — so the arch leans slightly backward. Gives the shape a body-like quality (weight forward, head slightly extended, like someone in mid-stride).
- **Stroke:** 22px on 256px canvas (same weight as Way2Fly for visual consistency across the family).
- **Joins:** rounded caps and joins — matches the warm/humanist feel.
- **No rotation.** Way2Fly tilts -6° for forward momentum; Way2Move sits upright at 0° for groundedness. Same family, different posture.
- **Color:** Terracotta `#C4622D` on light surfaces. Warm Paper `#F5EFE7` when reversed.

**The mark reads as:**
1. A stylized "2" (brand recognition, Way2 family)
2. A ground + step + arc — the gait cycle, literally drawn (brand meaning)

### Tagline

**Primary:** "Train from the ground up."
**Secondary (for longer copy):** "The movement OS for normal-life athletes."

Primary goes under the logo lockup in the splash screen. It's five words. It says what we do (train) and the fundamental methodology (ground up — PRI/DNS, gait cycle). It's a command, not a claim.

## 6. Motion Language

| Intent | Duration | Curve | Where |
|---|---|---|---|
| Micro (taps, state changes) | 120ms | ease-out | Card selection, nav tap |
| Standard (navigation, list reveal) | 280ms | ease-out-quart | Screen transitions, bottom sheet |
| Settled (completion, summary) | 450ms | cubic-bezier(.2,.9,.3,1) | Assessment score reveal, session summary |
| Breath (journaling idle) | 2400ms loop | ease-in-out | Mic-is-listening pulse, subtle ambient states |
| Reward (milestone, goal complete) | 680ms | spring-soft | Soft Gold shimmer on completion |

**Principle — "Breath, not bounce."** Motion here evokes calm movement: a chest rising, a step landing, a settle. Nothing hyperactive. Springs are softened. Snaps are rare. Where Way2Fly springs with momentum, Way2Move arcs with intention.

## 7. Spacing & Sizing

**Base unit:** 4px.

| Token | Value | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| `sp-xs` | 4px | Icon-to-label gap |
| `sp-sm` | 8px | Tight pad |
| `sp-md` | 16px | Default pad / card pad |
| `sp-lg` | 24px | Section spacing |
| `sp-xl` | 32px | Major section break |
| `sp-2xl` | 48px | Screen-level breathing room |

**Radii:** 10 / 14 / 20 / 28. Slightly softer than Way2Fly's scale — warmer curves suit the brand. Cards are 14, hero cards are 20, modals are 28.

**Min tap target:** 48×48px, non-negotiable.

## 8. Iconography

**Style:** Outlined, rounded joins, 1.75px stroke on 24px grid. Slightly warmer than the typical 2px geometric set — aligns with Manrope/Fraunces tone.

**Source:** Phosphor Icons (Regular weight) as the primary library. For anatomy-specific icons not in Phosphor (pelvis view, knee valgus marker, gait phase diagram), custom SVGs are authored in the same style.

**Consistency rules:**
- Always outlined, never filled, except for:
  - Nav active state → filled version of the current tab icon
  - Severity status pills → filled circle
- Corner rounding on all icon strokes (round-cap)
- No drop shadows on icons
- No two-color icons inside the main UI (exception: the 5-emoji stomach-feeling selector, which is intentionally playful)

## Process notes

This plan was developed by following the reusable framework defined in Way2Fly's `design-process.md`:

1. **Discovery & Strategy** — Way2Move's positioning was clearer from the start (the full product specification already existed in `docs/GENERAL_PROJECT_SPECIFICATION.md`). Market audit focused on wellness/rehab competitors (Caliber, Future, Peloton Strength) and movement-education tools (GOWOD, Pliability). Honest assessment: differentiator is the PRI/DNS-rooted methodology plus voice-first journal, not the design.
2. **Visual Foundation** — inverted Way2Fly's defaults (light → dark, geometric → humanist, electric → earth) to build a distinct product identity while keeping the "Way2" family resemblance.
3. **Logo** — same construction principle as Way2Fly (single-stroke "2") but deliberately re-posed: upright instead of tilted, grounded base instead of swept arc, terracotta instead of teal.
4. **System** — applied tokens across key component families (buttons, cards, inputs, nav, pills, progress indicators) and demonstrated on 20 screens in `docs/screens/revamp-v1/design/mockups.html`.

Phase 5 (Application & Testing) will happen when screens are translated to Flutter — not part of this document.
